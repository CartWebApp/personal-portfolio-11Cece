Frankenstein Constructed Response During my second year at CART in Web Development, I took Honors 12 English and our first unit was on Frankenstein. During this unit, I was tasked with a constructed response where I would have at least 40 minutes to evaluate a prompt to show my understanding of the book. This constructed response was a way more to express my knowledge of literature and really my critical thinking skills. Being timed for this task was pressuring and it did make me nervous, however I managed to push through with everything that I had. The issue with this assessment was that it made me doubt myself of remembering the content of the book and how else I could better describe my answer. Resolving this issue for me was to just focus on one answer in the matter of this short time frame instead of constantly overthinking other answers. I added into the mixture everything that I knew and as a result of my work and cognitive thinking I received a “B” as my final grade. For this assessment, I will express my habits of mind I used during the duration of my work which was: to connect and persist. These two are my key points because I tried to connect my evidence with everything that I knew from the book or to connect with our society/ideas. I was persistent on sticking with my one answer and adding more onto that answer with more information and connections that I could think of that would relate or answer the prompt question.

Research Paper During CART, it was my first semester final. My class was collaborating with the UX design lab in partnering with the Fresno Chaffee Zoo to ease their working environment. I was assigned to create my own research question and write a research paper to answer my topic question. I decided to do my research category on cognitive and sensory enrichment. My overarching questions were: Can cognitive enrichment reduce stress-related behaviors and improve overall welfare, indirectly supporting cognitive development and adaptability? What are the long-term retention effects of cognitive enrichment once the enrichment is removed? Does cognitive enrichment influence levels of neurogenesis or neural plasticity markers? I’ve reached out to other local zoos to gather information and it was difficult while waiting for a response back from them through email. Unfortunately, I did not receive a response from any of the zoos. However, I was determined to research more about my category to take key notes of information I can use in my argument. My final grade for this writing assignment was a B+ and I was proud of myself either way for passing and working hard to achieve good progress with my skills in literature. My habits of mind when working on this research paper, was investigation and connection. I investigated finding resources and saving them on a google doc that I can use for reference and help me to get ideas for information I can use. I used connection to connect back to my resources and tie together key concepts and points to persuade my audience.
